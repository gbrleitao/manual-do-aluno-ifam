Meet Some Teachers

Reginaldo Simões

Teacher in the field of Humanities. Has experience in the field of Education, with an emphasis on Adult Education, primarily focusing on the following theme: society, work, and culture;

Holds degrees in three areas of Humanities and Social Sciences: History, Geography, and Philosophy.


Thiago Batista Moreira

EBTT Teacher at the Federal Institute of Amazonas, stationed at the Manacapuru Advanced Campus. Has expertise in the field of Metabolism, Biochemistry, and Plant Physiology, as well as in Metabolic Modeling and Microalgae;

Holds a degree in Biological Sciences, with a Master's and Doctorate in Botany from the University of Brasília.


Lorenzo Barroco

Has a Bachelor's degree in Fisheries Engineering and a Master's degree in Tropical Fisheries Sciences, both from UFAM;

Holds a Ph.D. in Animal Science and Fisheries Resources from UFAM, in partnership with the Geochronology Laboratory of the University of Brasília and BOREA, from the National Museum of Natural History in Paris, France (2019).


Jaidson Brandão

Teacher and Coordinator of the Computer Science Course at IFAM. He is a leader and software engineer for Research and Development (R&D) projects at IFAM;

Holds a degree in Information Systems, a specialization in Software Engineering, and a master's degree in Process Engineering. He has previously worked in a Federal Autarchy with Databases in the Judiciary; TJAM-Systems Analyst.


Walter Claudino

EBTT Teacher- Computer Science at the Federal Institute of Education, Science, and Technology of Amazonas;

Holds a Bachelor's degree in Information Systems, with specialization in Didactics and Higher Education Methodology. Has experience in the field of Computer Science, with emphasis on Computer Systems.


Gabriel Leitão

Holds a Bachelor's degree in Computer Engineering from the Federal University of Amazonas (UFAM 2009);

And in 2023, obtained a Ph.D. in Informatics from UFAM.


Hilton Barros

Computer Science professor at the Federal Institute of Education, Science, and Technology of Amazonas, Manacapuru Advanced Campus;
